# Controlled Demo Guide

Use this guide to present BuildingOS in a controlled, professional way to a building administrator or prospect. The goal is to show value clearly without exposing internal details, unfinished modules, or development-only language.

## Quick path

1. Open the landing page and explain the business problem BuildingOS solves.
2. Offer the guided demo first for most prospects.
3. Use the interactive demo only when the person wants to explore on their own.
4. Close with a clear next step: contact, follow-up call, or pilot conversation.

## Recommended walkthrough

| Step | What to show | Why it matters |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| 1 | Landing page | Explain the problem, the value proposition, and the two demo paths. |
| 2 | Guided demo page | Set expectations and tell the story in a controlled order. |
| 3 | Administration dashboard | Show how the platform gives the operator a single operational view. |
| 4 | Buildings, units, and residents | Show structure, tenant scope, and operational organization. |
| 5 | Finances | Show charges, balances, liquidations, and payment tracking. |
| 6 | Payments and proofs | Show payment submission and proof handling. |
| 7 | Documents | Show building/unit documents and resident-visible files. |
| 8 | Resident portal | Show the resident experience: debt, payments, documents, and requests. |
| 9 | Requests / tickets | Show operational follow-up and tracking. |
| 10 | Close | Confirm fit, answer questions, and schedule the next step. |

## Strong areas to highlight

- Admin dashboard and operational overview.
- Buildings, units, residents, and tenant scoping.
- Finances: balances, payments, liquidations, and proof review.
- Resident portal with a clear, limited scope.
- Documents and requests tracking.

## Areas to avoid highlighting too early

- Commercial billing and subscription complexity.
- AI features or future roadmap items.
- Beta-wide claims or production-scale promises.
- Self-service claims that are not fully closed yet.
- Anything that sounds like an internal test environment.

## Demo guardrails

- Say clearly that the demo uses sample data.
- Do not show passwords, secrets, or internal credentials.
- Do not mention seed commands, database resets, or operational shortcuts.
- Do not open with technical implementation details.
- If a module is weak or still evolving, frame it as roadmap instead of core value.

## Suggested closing script

“BuildingOS helps an administrator keep buildings, residents, finances, payments, documents, and requests in one place. The platform is ready for a controlled pilot conversation, and we can tailor the next walkthrough to your operation.”

## Checklist

- [ ] The demo path is clear before the walkthrough starts.
- [ ] No internal credentials are visible.
- [ ] The story follows admin → finances → resident experience.
- [ ] Weak roadmap items are not overpromised.
- [ ] The close includes a real next step.
